ABOUT ME
I am interested by the many forces behind species divergence in sympatry. During my PhD, I specifically showed how differences in temporal patterns alone could generate speciation. To do so, I employed a mix of field experiments and theoritical models. I am thus particularly interested in the interactions between chronobiology and speciation. As a personal preference, I'd love to work in polar regions (Artic + Antarctic), and study how the drastic changes in day lenght could shift gene flow over large ranges.
